Covid 19 Modelling & Database

We utilized compartmental models to compare and comprehend the transmission dynamics and epidemiology of two infectious diseases: SARS-CoV-2 and SARS-CoV-1. By analyzing crucial parameters such as the basic reproduction number, transmission modes, and duration of the infectious period, we aimed to gain insights into how these viruses spread within populations. These insights were then used to inform public health interventions aimed at controlling their transmission. Additionally, we created a comprehensive database of cases to facilitate our analysis and provide a valuable resource for future research and surveillance efforts.

Antibiotic Resistance in E.coli

Our Study delves into the alarming rise of antibiotic resistance caused by rampant drug misuse and its implications on treating pathogenic bacterial colonies. We conducted an experiment comparing the effectiveness of conventional medicines and natural herbs in treating an E. coli culture. While azithromycin proved to be the most effective, a few resistant strains were observed. Natural herbs like tea tree oil showed promising results with minimal resistance. This study offers insights into alternative treatments and resistance mechanisms, highlighting the need for further research and preventative measures.

Unpacking the Mind

"Unpacking the Mind: An Analogy of Human Memory Through Alvin and the Chipmunks" is an informative and engaging article that uses the popular cartoon characters Alvin and the Chipmunks to explain the complex processes of human memory. The article breaks down the workings of the brain's memory system, drawing parallels to the musical talents and antics of the beloved chipmunk trio. The author presents a clear and concise explanation of the different types of memory, and how they are formed and stored in the brain. Through this unique analogy, readers gain a better understanding of how memory works and how they can improve their own memory retention.

Covid 19 and Vaccines

Influenza Vaccines as a potential alternate to Covid vaccines, in developing countries with limited access to m-RNA Covid Vaccines. The Rising Incidents of Sars-Cov-2 among children between the age of 12-16, has been increasing at an exponential rate in recent years, and since it is accompanied by the immediate need for physical education, children are at no doubt among the high risk groups. The main purpose of the research is to investigate the current opinions of children between the age of 12 to 18 with regards to the impact of Covid on their Education and what they sense as the need of the hour. Using data analysis, this study analyzed the opinions among children from around 5 countries including India, United States, Zimbabwe, Thailand and Singapore and 4 states of India including Meghalaya, Arunachal Pradesh, New Delhi, and Assam. The efficacy of online education was also measured on a metric scale of 0 to 6, with 0 being the lowest and 6 being the highest. As hypothesized, it was found that opinions varied, with respect to certain geographical regions. However, it was also found out that most children between 12 to 15 years of age were facing undue stress, and around 60% of these children reported an estimated 10 hours of screen time per day, which is far above the advisable 5-6 hours per day. This study answers the questions on solutions to the current problem and suggestions that these children consider accurate.

Ecoimmunology

This meta-analysis investigates the relationship between habitat quality and immune function in the eastern quoll (Dasyurus viverrinus), an endangered marsupial species in Australia. We conducted a comprehensive literature search and identified 10 studies that met our inclusion criteria. Our results show that habitat quality has a significant impact on the immune function of eastern quolls, with individuals in degraded habitats exhibiting reduced immune function and increased susceptibility to disease. We also found evidence that habitat fragmentation and loss of genetic diversity may further exacerbate the negative effects of habitat degradation on immune function. Overall, our findings suggest that habitat conservation and restoration efforts are crucial for the long-term survival and health of the eastern quoll and other endangered species. Further research is needed to better understand the underlying mechanisms of this relationship and develop effective conservation strategies.
